# Participant Methodology

## Overview
This section describes the methodology used to involve participants in the usability research of the system.

## Participant Selection
Participants were strictly selected based on the primary demographic: **Graduates and external professionals seeking diploma courses or continuing education**. Because this group combines demanding professional careers with academic study, the selection deliberately excluded regular undergraduate students to ensure findings accurately reflect the mental models and constraints of busy professionals.

## Sample Size
Following the recommendations of Jakob Nielsen and Thomas K. Landauer (1993), a sample size of **5 participants per distinct user group** will be used for formative usability testing. This specific number is statistically sufficient to discover approximately 85% of the most severe usability problems before iterating the design, optimizing the ROI (Return on Investment) of the evaluation phase.

## Participation Methods
Participants will contribute through the following formal HCI evaluation methods, designed to respect their limited availability:

- **Heuristic Evaluation (Expert Inspection):** Prior to user testing, 2-3 team members will evaluate the interfaces using Nielsen's 10 Usability Heuristics to catch obvious flaws and avoid wasting the professionals' time.
- **Moderated Usability Testing (Think-Aloud Protocol):** Agile 20-minute sessions where participants narrate their thoughts while attempting predefined tasks. These sessions are kept intentionally brief to respect the participants' demanding work schedules.
- **System Usability Scale (SUS) Questionnaire:** Immediately following the usability test, the industry-standard 10-item Likert scale questionnaire will be administered to obtain a quantitative usability score (Target: > 68 points).

## Ethical Considerations
All participants will be informed about the purpose of the study and their participation will be voluntary. Any collected information will be used only for research purposes.
